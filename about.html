About

I'm Guilherme Raul Cosentino Ferreira. I test autonomous vehicle perception systems at NVIDIA. Before that I spent three years at Stellantis building simulation pipelines for ADAS and writing HVAC embedded software. I started an MS in Computer Science specializing in AI at Georgia Tech, but paused it to join NVIDIA.

I grew up in Recife, Brazil. I live in Sunnyvale, California.

I'm interested in how systems understand things — and what "understanding" even means. That pulls me toward linguistics, natural language processing, information theory, and measurement theory. The thread connecting them: how do you quantify something as slippery as meaning, consciousness, or intelligence? I don't think you can neatly. But I think the attempt to measure it tells you something about the thing you're measuring, and about yourself.

We don't know the exact moment chemistry becomes biology, or the moment matter starts caring about its own continuation. But self-replicating procedures — whether RNA molecules or software agents — are part of that story. If you think of living things as biological machines that reproduce themselves, it's not far-fetched to say we're self-replicating code on wet hardware. Theoretically, you could restructure matter to perform the same functions — that's science fiction, except we already have models that outperform most humans at specific cognitive tasks. The line between "processes information" and "understands" is the line I keep trying to locate.
