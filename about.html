About

I'm Guilherme Raul Cosentino Ferreira. I test autonomous vehicle perception systems at NVIDIA. Before that I spent three years at Stellantis building simulation pipelines for ADAS and writing HVAC embedded software. I started an MS in Computer Science specializing in AI at Georgia Tech, but paused it to join NVIDIA.

I grew up in Recife, Brazil. I live in Sunnyvale, California.

I'm interested in what understanding is and where it comes from. That pulls me toward linguistics, natural language processing, information theory, measurement theory, and critical consciousness — the idea that real awareness means becoming aware of the systems you're embedded in, not just operating within them. These aren't separate interests. They're the same question from different angles: how does matter organize itself into something that knows it's there? No single neuron understands English. Somehow, neurons do. Understanding isn't localized — it's a property of organization. So the real question is what kind of organization is sufficient, and whether the substrate matters at all.

We don't know the exact moment chemistry becomes biology, or when self-replicating matter starts caring about its own continuation. But the pattern keeps showing up — in RNA molecules, in neural networks, in the systems I test at work. If cognition is computation, not every computation is cognition. But we already have models that outperform most humans at specific cognitive tasks, and the line between "processes information" and "understands" keeps moving. I'm trying to locate it. I wrote about some of these ideas here.
